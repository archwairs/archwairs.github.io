Returning to Writing

February 2026

It has been so long since I last sat down and wrote something. I have been writing text messages to ChatGPT, as a way to ground myself, but that doesn’t really count as writing in my opinion.

It has been about fifteen days since my new semester has started, and I am already starting to feel a little burnt out, even though the semester hasn’t demanded much apart from showing up to classes.

Before this, I was at home for a couple of weeks for my winter break. That was good rest. I didn’t do any formal work. I woke up late, had good food, hung out with my friends, and finished reading Men Without Women by Murakami. It was a good read, and it made me want to watch Drive My Car, which shares its title with the first short story in the book and is loosely based on it.

It has been a while since I watched a movie in one go by myself. I just can’t relax enough to completely focus on the movie. There are multiple things going on in my head, questions that feel like they need instant answering.

There are so many classic movies that I want to watch, but I am unable to make the space for them. It is easier for me to watch a movie when I am with someone, which I think is partly because I watched so many great movies growing up with my brother, and then at university with my ex.

I haven’t even read anything since I came back after the break. I keep dismissing the idea, telling myself that I don’t have the energy, or that I am not in the mood. I don’t know why, but sitting quietly and doing something is almost scary to me.

Even the studying I have done since coming back has been through lectures or ChatGPT, giving me a false sense of companionship. I keep unlocking my phone and switching between the same few apps, hoping for a new update of some sort.

I keep doing chess puzzles on my phone, trying to use my brain in the most pressure-less way I can think of.

I don’t understand when things became so heavy. I am definitely doing better than I did a couple of months ago, but somewhere along the way it seems that I have lost the energy and drive I remember having during my undergrad, even during the beginning of my master’s.

Part of me likes to think that I am worth nothing by myself, that I need someone to prop me up and make me do my best work. And maybe there is some truth to that.

But I also realize that the only one I can truly rely on, long term, is myself. So I need to do something. Maybe I just need more time. Maybe things will get better.
